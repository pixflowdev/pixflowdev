# Bento Component Roles

## Hero Card

- Largest card in the first board.
- Contains badge, H1, lead, and actions.
- Uses a subtle gradient or tint, not a loud decorative background.
- Must feel like a complete landing hero inside the card.

## Capability Card

- Small or medium card explaining one sharp product advantage.
- Uses one icon mark, title, and short body.
- Example roles: speed, collaboration, builder-ready output, security, automation.

## Metric Card

- Contains a label, large value, and a small trend/status pill.
- Use realistic placeholders only when no source data exists.
- Include supporting context so the number is not ornamental.

## Accent Promise Card

- Large lower card that carries a strong statement or product visualization.
- Often uses the main accent gradient or saturated brand field.
- Should add emotional weight without overwhelming the whole page.

## Checklist Card

- Medium/tall card with a short heading and 4 to 6 rows.
- Each row uses a consistent check icon, label, and optional subtext.
- Good for enterprise readiness, setup workflow, or quality gates.

## Quote Card

- Soft card with short human quote or role-based note.
- Prefer generic role-based attribution unless real names were provided.
- Use a large decorative quote mark only if it stays behind the text and does not reduce contrast.

## Community Or Action Card

- Compact card for docs, community, onboarding, support, or next step.
- May include one simple arrow or pill action.
- Should not duplicate the main CTA.

## Product Mockup Card

- Large card in the second board.
- Contains labeled rows, tabs, panels, charts, device frame, or builder states.
- The labels must match the product category from the brief.

## Card Shell Rules

- Minimum width: in the main desktop board, no visible card may occupy only 1 or 2 of 12 tracks.
- Compact signal cards may use 3 of 12 tracks only if they contain a short label, one metric, or one icon plus one line.
- Any card with body copy, a quote, checklist rows, action buttons, badges, or product UI needs at least 4 of 12 tracks.
- Use nested rows, chips, mini grids, or a new row inside a wide card instead of creating skinny standalone cards.
- Radius: 24px to 36px for large cards; 18px to 24px for small cards.
- Padding: generous; card content should not touch edges.
- Borders: subtle tinted borders, not heavy outlines.
- Shadows: soft and broad; avoid harsh drop shadows.
- Color: use the accent sparingly and reserve saturated fills for one or two anchor cards.
- Contrast: saturated cards with light text must use explicit dark-enough backgrounds or overlays at desktop and mobile sizes. Avoid light text directly on pale peach, mint, or blue tints.
