# Paper Layouts

Use these patterns as the route contract. Adapt the subject and copy to the user's brief, but preserve the document-workspace rhythm.

## 1. Light Product Nav

Purpose: establish calm product credibility.

Structure:

- Full-width top bar with subtle bottom border.
- Inner container max around 1440px.
- Brand on left with a small document mark.
- Four short center links on desktop.
- Sign-in text and compact blue/ink pill CTA on the right.

Rules:

- Keep nav height around 64px to 76px.
- Hide or compress center links before they crowd the brand.
- Do not add a heavy glass header, logo wall, or colorful badge row.

## 2. Document Workspace Hero

Purpose: make Paper recognizable in the first viewport.

Preferred desktop structure:

- One-column or gentle left-aligned hero intro, max text width around 760px to 880px.
- Large display heading at clamp(48px, 5vw, 76px), weight 500, line-height about 1.05.
- Supporting paragraph max 62ch.
- Two pill CTAs.
- Large editor/workspace shell below or beside the intro, but only if both columns remain wide.

Guardrails:

- If the editor shell would force the headline into narrow line breaks, stack the shell below the intro.
- Do not use a classic SaaS split with a tiny mockup.
- The editor shell should appear near the fold and be visibly wider than a card.

## 3. Editor Shell Proof

Purpose: replace generic screenshots with inspectable product proof.

Structure:

- White outer frame with top chrome.
- Left sidebar with document groups or workspace labels.
- Main document pane with title, status, metadata, heading, paragraphs, rows, and an AI/comment/suggestion card.
- Optional small right utility chip or collaborator row.

Rules:

- Use real labels and small UI details, not lorem ipsum.
- Keep the shell readable at 1600px and simplified at mobile.
- Blur, fade, or crop only decorative lower shell areas; essential labels stay legible.

## 4. Platform Thesis Section

Purpose: explain what the product changes.

Structure:

- Left or centered eyebrow.
- H2 max width 760px to 920px.
- Paragraph max 70ch.
- Three foundation cards below.

Rules:

- This section can be one column. Do not force a split if it compresses copy.
- Cards should support the thesis with writing, collaboration, AI, search, permissions, or publishing behaviors.

## 5. Six-Feature Card Grid

Purpose: show product breadth without becoming generic.

Structure:

- Section intro.
- Six white cards in a 3x2 desktop grid, 2x3 tablet, 1 column mobile.
- Each card includes a miniature UI plate: search bar, version rows, permission list, collaboration cursors, code well, AI suggestion, or document block.

Rules:

- Never create four desktop columns for Paper feature cards; cards become too thin.
- Each card minimum useful width should be about 320px.
- Miniatures should be quiet and pale, not colorful illustrations.

## 6. Workflow Row

Purpose: communicate how a team moves from idea to published artifact.

Structure:

- Four columns with large pale numbers, compact titles, and short copy.
- Optional thin connector arrows or rules between steps.

Rules:

- Keep numbers decorative but legible.
- On mobile, stack as cards or rows; do not keep four columns.

## 7. Use-Case Panel Set

Purpose: show where the document workspace applies.

Structure:

- One wide horizontal card first, then two smaller cards below.
- Left side: label, H3, paragraph.
- Right side: document/checklist/code/campaign miniature.

Rules:

- The wide card should feel like a real template preview.
- Use case miniatures must be product-specific and readable.

## 8. Advantage Split

Purpose: summarize trust and product reasons without another card grid.

Structure:

- Left: eyebrow, H2, paragraph.
- Right: vertical list with icon circles and body copy.

Rules:

- Use this split only if both columns remain wide. Otherwise stack the headline first, list second.
- Do not make this a dense KPI strip.

## 9. Pricing And Final CTA

Purpose: convert without breaking the calm paper rhythm.

Structure:

- Three pricing cards, one dark or stronger highlighted plan if conversion is needed.
- Final centered CTA below pricing or before footer.
- Footer with brand summary and four columns.

Rules:

- Highlighted plan can be dark; the rest of the page remains light.
- Buttons must fit on mobile.
- Keep footer light unless the user asks for a dark close.
