# Luxury Final Critique

Score the artifact before handoff.

## Composition

- Pass: oversized type, image composition, and whitespace create a controlled first viewport.
- Repair if: the hero is only text, only a card, or a generic split SaaS layout.
- Repair if: a two-column section makes a large heading, paragraph, image, or card feel squeezed. Use a centered/full-width intro, wider asymmetric tracks, local type scale adjustment, moved body copy, or a one-column stack.

## Typography

- Pass: uppercase display type is readable and intentional across breakpoints.
- Repair if: headings clip, paragraphs are too thin, or labels wrap awkwardly.

## Imagery

- Pass: images or CSS-built frames feel curated and support the offer.
- Repair if: imagery is generic decoration or absent from the first viewport.

## Luxury Restraint

- Pass: limited palette, sparse CTAs, measured rules, and quiet detail.
- Repair if: the page uses too much gold, too many cards, heavy gradients, or hype copy.

## Conversion Clarity

- Pass: the user knows what is offered and how to act.
- Repair if: the page reads as a moodboard without an action.

## Responsive

- Pass: mobile preserves hierarchy and avoids overflow.
- Repair if: image stacks, huge text, or pill labels overflow.
