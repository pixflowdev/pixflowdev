# Luxury Recipe

Use this recipe when the brief calls for a premium fashion, interior, fragrance, hospitality, jewellery, watch, gallery, private membership, high-end service, or refined product page that should feel quiet, expensive, and visually assured.

## Intent

Create a dark luxury landing page built from restraint: large uppercase typography, monochrome or near-monochrome color, dramatic but controlled product imagery, spacious section rhythm, precise microcopy, and a small number of deliberate calls to action.

This is a visual method, not a replacement for the active Pixflow Design skill. Follow the run brief, uploaded references, selected design system, craft rules, and active Pixflow skill first. Use this recipe to shape typography, contrast, image treatment, pacing, and premium component behavior.

## Core Rules

- Keep the page dark, disciplined, and high contrast. Use off-white text on a deep ink surface, with one muted metallic or warm accent only when it improves hierarchy.
- Lead with a first viewport that combines oversized uppercase type and a large product/image composition, but keep both sides wide enough to breathe at desktop scale.
- Use type contrast through weight, scale, case, and spacing rather than color variety.
- Let empty space feel intentional. Avoid filling every area with cards or copy.
- Use a mono or condensed display family when it supports the brief. Google Fonts such as Oswald, Geist Mono, JetBrains Mono, Archivo, or similar free families are acceptable when they match the quality target.
- Make imagery feel curated: portrait crops, object still-life, gallery frames, layered product panels, or editorial image stacks.
- Keep CTAs sparse. Primary action may be white on dark; secondary actions should be outline/text-based.
- Use subtle reveals or image-mask transitions only when the static page remains fully usable.

## Signature Page Rhythm

1. Minimal header with short wordmark, few links, and one quiet action.
2. Hero with small capsule label, oversized uppercase headline, image stack or dominant product visual, concise copy, and one primary CTA.
3. Editorial product/edit section with one large image frame and a text column.
4. Materials, craft, or provenance section with sparse proof blocks and measured rules.
5. Collection or offer section with two to four large cards, not a dense catalog grid.
6. Private-access, appointment, membership, or inquiry CTA that feels selective rather than salesy.
7. Footer with calm link columns, small legal text, and a restrained oversized wordmark or line.

## Visual DNA

- Background: deep ink, blue-black, near-black, or very dark aubergine. Avoid pure flat black if it makes sections feel dead.
- Text: off-white, pearl, or soft ivory.
- Accent: muted champagne, pale silver, graphite, or soft warm stone. Use sparingly.
- Typography: uppercase display, large scale, measured letter spacing, weight contrast.
- Layout: large margins, editorial image blocks, selective two-column alternation, restrained dividers.
- Desktop target: review at a 1600px viewport with a primary content width around 1440px. Do not design as if the page is only 1280px wide unless the brief demands a narrower editorial measure.
- Radius: mostly square or softly rounded image frames. Avoid bubbly SaaS radii.
- Borders: hairline white or ivory at low opacity.
- Shadows: minimal. Depth comes from contrast, crop, and scale.
- Motion: slow reveal, opacity, translate, or mask only. No bouncy animation.

## Copy Direction

Use language like collection, atelier, private viewing, crafted, material, edition, appointment, archive, provenance, signature, capsule, gallery, detail, finish, and commission.

Write specific, restrained copy. Avoid hype, discount language, emojis, fake scarcity claims, and visible references to external recipe sources, test harnesses, Bricks, WordPress, JSON, conversion, or internal workflow.

## Responsive Rules

- At desktop width, two-column luxury sections must not make the heading or body column feel like an accidental sidebar. If the heading becomes one-word-per-line or the paragraph measure feels cramped, repair the composition.
- Preferred repairs: use a full-width or centered intro above the image/cards, reduce the local display heading scale, widen the copy track, move body copy beneath the heading, or stack image and copy into one controlled column.
- Keep two columns when both columns have real purpose and enough width; do not collapse every split by default.
- The hero headline must shrink without clipping. Prefer 2 to 5 line breaks over viewport-width font scaling.
- Image stacks collapse into a single dominant image plus one small supporting tile on mobile.
- Two-column editorial sections stack with image first when visual context is required.
- Preserve 44px minimum touch targets and strong focus-visible states.
- Never let uppercase labels or navigation links overflow their containers.
- Avoid huge desktop margins on mobile; keep the page premium through spacing rhythm, not empty unreachable gaps.

## Bricks Awareness

Stage A should output clean HTML/CSS only. Do not plan Bricks JSON here.

- Use semantic sections and reusable classes.
- Keep decorative imagery, masks, and rules as CSS or simple HTML.
- Do not rely on external proprietary assets.
- Avoid fragile absolute-positioned text over imagery unless contrast is guaranteed.
- Keep JavaScript optional and scoped. This recipe usually does not need JavaScript.
