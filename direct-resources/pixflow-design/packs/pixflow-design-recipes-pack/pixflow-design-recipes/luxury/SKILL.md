# Luxury Recipe Skill

## Mission

Create a polished luxury web prototype that feels selective, quiet, and expensive without becoming vague, empty, or purely decorative.

## Required Behavior

- Start with a recognizable luxury first viewport: large uppercase headline, small capsule label, curated image/product composition, short copy, and one strong action.
- Use a dark premium surface and off-white text with strong contrast.
- Use one accent role at most: champagne, silver, graphite, or warm stone.
- Use imagery as a central design material. If no assets are supplied, create CSS-built editorial frames, product silhouettes, material panels, or generated assets when runtime image generation is available and useful.
- Preserve the recipe below the fold through editorial image/text alternation, craft/provenance proof, collection cards, private-access CTA, and refined footer.
- Make every section feel composed. No generic SaaS feature grids, floating metric cards, or noisy gradient decoration.
- Use the 1600px desktop review width well. Do not keep a two-column section if it compresses a display heading, paragraph, image frame, or card content into a skinny track.
- Repair compressed splits through a centered/full-width intro, asymmetric columns, local heading scale reduction, moved copy, or a one-column stack.

## Component Expectations

- Navigation is minimal and quiet.
- Buttons are sparse, uppercase or title-case, and have visible focus states.
- Cards are large, image-led, and few in number.
- Labels are small, measured, and useful.
- Image frames use disciplined crop, scale, and contrast.
- Dividers are thin and controlled.
- Footer is refined and not dense.

## Accessibility

- Maintain WCAG-friendly contrast for all text.
- Avoid low-opacity text below readable contrast.
- Give every interactive element a focus-visible style.
- Keep text selectable and real; do not bake core copy into images.
- Respect reduced motion.
- Do not use hover-only content disclosure.

## Anti-Drift

Do not drift into:

- generic black SaaS
- casino/gold excess
- fashion moodboard without page structure
- low-contrast grey-on-black
- random serif editorial poster
- dense ecommerce catalog grid
- luxury buzzwords with no offer clarity

## Final Check

Before handoff, verify:

- The page reads as luxury from the first viewport.
- The offer, audience, and primary action are clear.
- Typography is controlled and not clipping.
- Desktop splits do not squeeze headings, copy, or media.
- Imagery supports the hierarchy.
- Mobile preserves the premium rhythm.
- Visible copy does not mention external recipe sources or internal Pixflow workflow details.
