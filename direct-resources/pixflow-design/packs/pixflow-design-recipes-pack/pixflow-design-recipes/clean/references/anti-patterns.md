# Clean Anti-Patterns

Avoid these common failures:

- A white page that feels unfinished because there is no product proof.
- Generic "Everything you need" feature tiles with no visible product states.
- Six or more equal feature cards when two to four stronger cards would communicate more.
- Fake metric cards, random charts, unlabeled dashboard bars, or meaningless bento clutter.
- Dark theme drift. Clean is a light product method unless the user explicitly asks otherwise.
- Purple gradient dominance. Accent color should guide action, not decorate every section.
- Overly large shadows, glassmorphism, blobs, or abstract 3D shapes.
- Serif display headings, editorial mastheads, paper texture, brutalist outlines, and collage.
- Skinny columns at desktop width.
- CTA rows that wrap awkwardly.
- Badges or chips that extend past card edges.
- Product mockups where the text is too small to inspect.
- App-store button styling on a page that is not app-oriented.
- Real company names used as fake endorsements.

If any of these appear in the Stage A artifact, repair before handoff.
