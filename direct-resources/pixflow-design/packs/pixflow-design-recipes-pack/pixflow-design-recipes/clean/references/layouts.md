# Clean Layouts

Use these as layout patterns. Adapt copy, counts, and section order to the run brief. Do not copy preview screenshots section-for-section.

## Centered Product Hero

Purpose: instant clarity, premium restraint, and clean conversion intent.

Structure:

- Header with brand left, 3 to 4 center links, sign-in text link, and one small rounded CTA.
- Centered hero copy with a large H1, short lead, and two actions.
- Optional trust line or logo strip below the actions.
- Optional subtle background glow or soft radial tint, never a heavy gradient theme.

Quality bar:

- The H1 must name the outcome, not just the category.
- The section should feel intentionally sparse, not unfinished.
- Hero copy should use two to four readable lines on desktop.
- Avoid hero split layouts unless the screenshot/brief demands it.
- Use sans-serif type for every headline. Serif or magazine-style display type is a failure for this recipe.

## Hero Plus Product Slab

Purpose: make the product tangible before the user reaches feature cards.

Structure:

- Light hero section above.
- Large centered product slab below the hero or in the next band.
- Product slab can be browser/editor, dashboard, app screen, card stack, command palette, or builder workspace.
- Include tabs, side navigation, labels, rows, status chips, tooltips, suggestions, or progress states.

Rules:

- The slab should be at least 65 percent of the container width on desktop.
- Use a wide container up to 1440px for large product proof.
- Do not use a generic chart with unlabeled bars.
- Do not crop text inside the product proof.

## Logo Or Trust Strip

Purpose: add credibility without creating clutter.

Structure:

- Thin divider above or below.
- Small uppercase label.
- Four to eight muted brand/product/platform names or abstract logo marks.

Rules:

- Keep logos muted and secondary.
- Do not invent hard claims such as "SOC 2 certified" unless supplied.
- Hide or wrap gracefully on mobile.

## Asymmetric Feature Cards

Purpose: show capability depth without becoming noisy.

Structure:

- Centered section intro with small accent label.
- Grid of two to four large cards.
- Use one wider card for the most important capability and smaller cards for secondary capabilities.
- Cards include mini UI states, command rows, code snippets, lists, or state chips where useful.

Rules:

- At 1600px, every text card must have enough width for comfortable paragraphs.
- Avoid equal-emphasis six-card grids unless the brief needs a catalog.
- If a grid creates skinny cards, use two columns, one wide plus one narrow, or stack.

## Compact Feature Grid

Purpose: cover a second tier of functionality.

Structure:

- Soft grey section.
- Two-column or three-column card grid.
- Each card has a small icon tile, title, and one useful sentence.

Rules:

- Cards should be compact but not cramped.
- Icons must be simple and consistent.
- Avoid large decorative icons and repeated "save time" claims.

## Pricing Or Plan Cards

Purpose: make conversion concrete when the product is commercial.

Structure:

- Centered intro.
- Two or three plan cards.
- Price, audience, included points, CTA, and optional recommended badge.

Rules:

- Keep card heights balanced.
- Do not overstuff plan cards.
- Use the accent on the recommended plan only.
- If pricing is not relevant, replace with implementation steps, package tiers, or plan comparison.

## Testimonials Or Proof Cards

Purpose: support the promise with human or operational proof.

Structure:

- Centered intro.
- Three to six testimonial or proof cards.
- Each card includes quote/result, name/role or proof label, and a quiet divider.

Rules:

- Use believable placeholders if the brief gives no real proof.
- Do not invent named brands as factual endorsements.
- Use neutral cards and readable line lengths.

## Integrations / Stack Section

Purpose: show compatibility for tools, platforms, or workflows.

Structure:

- Centered intro.
- Logo/tile grid with known categories from the brief.
- Optional secondary row of pill labels.

Rules:

- Tiles should be simple and high-contrast.
- Keep the grid centered and avoid extra desktop columns that make tiles too narrow.

## Final CTA And Footer

Purpose: end with one direct next step.

Structure:

- Pale accent or white CTA band.
- Large centered heading, short reassurance, and one or two actions.
- Footer with brand summary and grouped links.

Rules:

- Keep the final CTA visually lighter than the hero.
- Do not add an unrelated product mockup at the bottom.
- Footer should be useful but restrained.
