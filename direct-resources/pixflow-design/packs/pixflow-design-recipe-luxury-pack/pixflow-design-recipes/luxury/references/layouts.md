# Luxury Layout Patterns

## Hero With Image Stack

Purpose: make the method recognizable immediately.

- Full dark section.
- Minimal header above or inside hero.
- Small capsule label.
- Oversized uppercase headline, usually 2 to 4 lines.
- Large image stack or product/gallery composition near the headline.
- One primary CTA plus optional private-access/social-proof note.
- Keep the copy short.

Desktop pattern:

- Centered container up to 1440px inside a 1600px desktop review viewport.
- Hero image area 560-760px tall.
- Main image centered with one or two smaller overlapping frames.
- Headline below or beside the image depending on brief.
- If the headline sits beside imagery, both columns need enough width. If the heading wraps into an awkward skinny stack, center the hero copy above/below the media or reduce the local display scale.

Mobile pattern:

- Header becomes compact.
- Hero image height reduces to 360-480px.
- Supporting image tiles shrink or disappear.
- Headline breaks into readable lines.

## Editorial Split

- One large image frame and one text column.
- Text column includes eyebrow, H2, paragraph, one text action.
- Alternate image/text order in later sections.
- Keep section rhythm slow; do not stack many splits too tightly.
- If a two-column split creates a cramped text measure, convert the intro to one column, widen the copy track, or move the paragraph beneath the heading.

## Section Intro Guardrail

For collection/proof/introduction sections, prefer one full-width intro block followed by the card/media grid when the heading is large or uppercase.

Use a side-by-side intro only when:

- the heading can hold strong line breaks without becoming a narrow vertical stack;
- the copy column has a comfortable measure;
- the split makes the section clearer than a centered or full-width intro.

If those conditions fail, collapse the intro to one column while preserving the lower card/media structure.

## Collection Cards

- Two to four cards only.
- Cards are image-led with short title, category, and one line of copy.
- Use large card dimensions and generous whitespace.
- Avoid dense ecommerce catalog grids.
- Card grids should not add extra desktop tracks that make cards too thin. Three cards may use three wide columns inside the 1440px container; four cards should use a 2x2 or asymmetric layout when card copy needs more width.

## Craft / Provenance Strip

- Hairline-separated rows or three measured proof blocks.
- Use material, process, edition, archive, or service proof.
- Keep numbers plausible and restrained.

## Private Access CTA

- Dark or slightly lifted panel.
- Short invitation headline.
- Appointment, consultation, private viewing, or membership action.
- Avoid urgent discount language.

## Footer

- Quiet wordmark and two to four link groups.
- Use small text, hairline top border, and generous padding.
- Optional oversized low-opacity word or mark is acceptable.
