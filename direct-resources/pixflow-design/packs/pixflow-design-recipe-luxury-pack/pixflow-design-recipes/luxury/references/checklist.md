## Quality Checks

- First viewport is recognizably luxury, not generic dark SaaS.
- Desktop was reviewed at 1600px width with about 1440px content max.
- The page has a clear offer, audience, and primary action.
- Typography is uppercase and expressive without clipping.
- No two-column section compresses a heading, paragraph, image frame, or card into an awkward skinny track.
- Section intros use one-column/centered layout when a side-by-side intro would make the heading or copy too narrow.
- Image/product composition is central to the design.
- Lower sections continue the recipe language.
- CTAs are sparse and premium.
- Mobile is polished and does not overflow.
- Text contrast remains readable.
- Focus states are visible.
- Visible copy does not mention external recipe sources, test harnesses, Bricks conversion, or internal file names.

## Final Questions

- Would a cropped first-viewport screenshot communicate premium/luxury immediately?
- Does the page feel restrained rather than empty?
- Do the hero and section intros use the full desktop canvas without squeezing the content?
- Are image frames curated and purposeful?
- Is the conversion action refined instead of salesy?
- Did any lower section drift into generic features, metrics, or card clutter?
