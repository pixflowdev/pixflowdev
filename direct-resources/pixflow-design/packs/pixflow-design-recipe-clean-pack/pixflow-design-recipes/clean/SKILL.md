# Clean Design Skill

## Mission

Create a production-quality Pixflow Design Stage A artifact in the Clean method. The result must feel minimal, premium, legible, spacious, useful, and commercially credible.

## Design Intent

Clean removes competing signals so the product promise, proof, and action path become obvious. Use whitespace, calm hierarchy, useful product evidence, and a tight palette to make the page feel practical and production-ready.

## Foundations

- Visual style: minimal, product-led, and clean.
- Typography: Poppins, Lato, Inter, Roboto, or system sans. Use Inconsolata or ui-monospace only for small labels, code, counters, or command text.
- Type scale: 12, 14, 16, 18, 20, 24, 32, 44, 64, with larger hero sizes derived from the active brief.
- Spacing: 8pt baseline grid.
- Colors: one blue or violet-blue primary, neutral white surfaces, near-black text, accessible muted copy, and status colors only for state meaning.
- Accessibility: WCAG 2.2 AA, keyboard-first interactions, visible focus states, semantic HTML before ARIA, reduced-motion support, and 44px+ touch targets.

## Rules

- Use a centered hero unless the user reference clearly requires another arrangement.
- Put inspectable product proof immediately after the hero or within the first product band.
- Prefer a large browser/app/editor/workspace slab before adding many small feature cards.
- Use trust logos, testimonials, pricing, integrations, or compatibility proof when the page is commercial.
- Prefer semantic tokens over raw values and keep accent use purposeful.
- Keep interaction states explicit: default, hover, focus-visible, active, disabled, loading, and error.
- Design empty, loading, and error states when the page includes product UI.
- Use one primary accent for CTAs and active states; avoid decorative color drift.
- Keep labels literal and short.
- Prioritize accessibility and clarity over novelty when uncertain.
- Keep cards readable. At a 1600px viewport, no important card should become too narrow for its text or UI state.
- Keep screenshots/reference images as visual guidance only. Do not trace, embed, or copy their exact layout.

## Anti-Patterns

- Low-contrast grey-on-white text.
- Inconsistent spacing rhythm.
- Decorative motion without purpose.
- Ambiguous labels.
- Random bento card clutter in a page that should feel simple.
- Heavy gradients, fake awards, generic metric cards, or repeated feature tiles.
- A hero that feels empty because no product proof appears soon after.
- Generic dashboards with unlabeled bars, fake metrics, or meaningless charts.
- Section grids that add extra columns and make cards too thin.
- Dark theme drift, editorial typography, serif display headings, and decorative collage.

## Acceptance Criteria

- The first viewport communicates the product outcome without clutter.
- Product proof appears quickly and is inspectable.
- The preview is a full page, not a simple card.
- The page remains recognizably Clean below the hero.
- All text is legible on mobile and no horizontal overflow appears.
- Buttons and links have clear interaction states.
- The final artifact feels like a real product page, not a theme demo.
- The artifact is standalone HTML/CSS, uses Pixflow-owned visible copy, and avoids external source branding.
