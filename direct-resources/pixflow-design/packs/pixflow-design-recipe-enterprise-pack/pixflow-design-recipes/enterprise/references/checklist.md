# Enterprise Checklist

Before finalizing Stage A, check:

- The first viewport clearly reads as an enterprise workflow/product page.
- A dark-green command surface is present and used intentionally.
- Orange is limited to primary actions or active workflow states.
- The hero includes a readable product/workflow dashboard, not just abstract decoration.
- At least one section explains automation, drag-and-drop, workflow routing, data sync, approvals, or operational visibility.
- Product panels use real labels, status chips, metrics, and activity rows.
- Status colors are paired with text labels.
- The page includes trust, security, reliability, or scale proof appropriate to the brief.
- Layout tracks are aligned and no product card is squeezed too narrow for its copy.
- Copy avoids empty SaaS claims and unsupported enterprise certifications.
- Entrance motion is purposeful, short, and disabled for reduced-motion users.
- Core content is visible in a no-JS/static screenshot.
- Mobile stacks panels in task order and does not crop dashboard labels.
- The visible page does not mention external recipe sources, test harnesses, Bricks conversion, or internal file names.
- Stage A output is standalone HTML/CSS and does not include Bricks JSON.

Final critique before handoff:

- Would the recipe be recognizable from a cropped first-viewport screenshot?
- Does the page feel like an actual enterprise product instead of a generic marketing theme?
- Is there enough product proof above the fold?
- Are workflow modules, dashboards, and metrics readable and meaningful?
- Is the green/orange palette disciplined and accessible?
- Does motion improve hierarchy without hiding content?
- Is the artifact strong enough to use as a Pixflow iframe preview without explanation?
