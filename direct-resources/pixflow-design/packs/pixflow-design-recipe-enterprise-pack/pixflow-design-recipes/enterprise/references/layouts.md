# Enterprise Layouts

Use these as layout patterns. Adapt copy, counts, and section order to the run brief.

## Command Hero With Workflow Dashboard

Purpose: make the recipe recognizable immediately.

Structure:

- Fixed or sticky dark-green topbar.
- Hero copy centered or split, with orange primary CTA and neutral secondary CTA.
- Large product dashboard under or beside the hero copy.
- Dashboard includes metrics, recent activity, status rows, workflow cards, and at least one URL/status label.
- Optional reveal motion: copy first, product surface second, internal metric cards third.

Rules:

- The hero product surface must be inspectable and relevant to the product.
- Do not use abstract laptop/device placeholders without real labels.
- Do not leave the first viewport as copy only. Enterprise needs product proof above the fold.
- Dark hero sections must maintain readable teal/white contrast.

## Workflow Builder Section

Purpose: show drag-and-drop or automation behavior.

Structure:

- Section intro with a short operational claim.
- Two-column layout: left copy/checklist, right workflow builder panel.
- Workflow builder contains trigger, condition, action, approval, sync, or AI-agent nodes.
- Use connectors, active states, and short row labels.

Rules:

- Nodes must be wide enough for labels.
- If the builder uses tabs, the selected tab must be visually clear.
- On mobile, convert the diagram into an ordered sequence of cards.
- Avoid unlabeled circles, random connector lines, and fake complex spaghetti diagrams.

## Platform Capability Grid

Purpose: cover enterprise feature breadth without generic filler.

Structure:

- Eyebrow and concise heading.
- Six cards max: execution, security, analytics, scale, integrations, AI automation.
- Each card includes a functional icon/mark, title, body, and optional status token.

Rules:

- Cards should be equal only within this specific grid. Do not repeat equal card grids across the whole page.
- Use concrete enterprise nouns and verbs.
- Orange should not appear in every card.

## Operations Visibility Split

Purpose: prove the system gives control over live work.

Structure:

- Left value copy with three proof rows.
- Right large dashboard/table/activity panel.
- Include metrics such as runs, saved hours, SLA, approvals, or syncs.

Rules:

- Tables need headers or row labels.
- Charts need legends or captions.
- Status colors must be paired with text labels.

## Enterprise Trust And Pricing

Purpose: close concerns for B2B buyers.

Structure:

- Trust/security band with SSO, audit logs, compliance, SLA, data controls.
- Pricing/plan cards only when the brief calls for them.
- Final CTA with implementation or demo language.

Rules:

- Keep trust language sober.
- Never invent certifications as real unless supplied. Use placeholder phrasing such as "SOC 2-ready controls" when needed.
- CTA must use the same green/orange visual system.
