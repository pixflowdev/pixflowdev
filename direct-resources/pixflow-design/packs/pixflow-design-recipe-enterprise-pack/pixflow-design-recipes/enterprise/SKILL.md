# Enterprise Design Skill

## Mission

Create implementation-ready guidance for a Pixflow Design Stage A artifact in a polished enterprise workflow style. The result should feel like a real operations product with strong hierarchy, believable product evidence, accessible states, and no generic SaaS filler.

## Design Intent

Enterprise is for data-heavy software, automation platforms, workflow builders, AI operations tools, B2B dashboards, and internal command centers. It balances a dark high-confidence control surface with warm readable product sections.

## Foundations

- Visual style: clean, high-contrast, structured, operational, enterprise-ready.
- Typography: Oswald-style display for concise high-impact headings; Ubuntu/Inter-style sans for body; Ubuntu Mono/JetBrains Mono-style labels for status and metadata.
- Color: primary deep green `#072C2C`, secondary orange `#FF5F03`, warm surface `#EDEADE`, near-black text `#111827`.
- Density: comfortable but information-rich. The page should have enough product detail to be trusted by technical and operations buyers.
- Accessibility: WCAG AA contrast, keyboard-first interaction cues, clear focus states, no color-only statuses.
- Motion: short reveal and hover transitions only, with reduced-motion support and static fallback visibility.

## Rules

- Define the product outcome in the hero and prove it with a real-looking workflow surface.
- Use deep green for header, hero, command surfaces, or footer. Use it as a serious control color, not a decorative wash.
- Use orange sparingly for primary CTA and selected/active workflow signals.
- Make product panels specific: labels, status chips, metrics, activity rows, connector names, and state changes.
- Include at least one automation or workflow model: triggers, connectors, conditions, queues, modules, runs, approvals, or audit steps.
- Keep layout tracks and panel edges aligned. Enterprise pages fail when card placement feels accidental.
- Make hover, focus-visible, active, disabled, loading, and error states explicit when interactive UI is represented.
- Pair any motion or visual affordance with real content. No decorative-only animation.

## Anti-Patterns

- Generic blue SaaS layout with no enterprise product surface.
- Bright orange used as a broad page background.
- Abstract node diagrams without labels, states, or workflow meaning.
- Decorative dashboards full of unlabeled rectangles.
- Low-contrast teal text on dark green.
- Too many equally weighted cards.
- Motion that hides content, scroll-jacks, parallax effects, or auto-rotating tabs.
- Copy that makes unsupported enterprise claims.

## Acceptance Criteria

- The first viewport is instantly recognizable as an enterprise automation/workflow product.
- Dark green and orange are used according to their roles.
- The product mockup has readable labels and believable states.
- At least one workflow/automation section explains how work moves through the product.
- Trust, security, scale, or reliability proof is included.
- Responsive layouts preserve content priority and never create horizontal overflow.
- Reduced-motion users and no-JS screenshots still see the full content.
- The artifact is strong enough to use as a Pixflow iframe preview without explanation.
