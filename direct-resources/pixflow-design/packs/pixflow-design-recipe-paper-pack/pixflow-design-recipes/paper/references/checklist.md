# Paper Checklist

## Required Reads Before Stage A Planning

- Read `design-method.md` for Paper intent and route rhythm.
- Read `method-skill.md` for runtime guardrails.
- Read `method-design-system.md` and `method-tokens.css` before choosing colors, radii, typography, or accent behavior.
- Read `method-layouts.md` and choose the page structure.
- Read `method-components.md` before building the editor shell, cards, pricing, or miniatures.
- Read `method-example.html` for structural evidence. Do not copy it verbatim.
- Read active Pixflow craft rules, especially pixel discipline, laws of UX, and typographic rhythm.

## Planning Checklist

- The page defaults to light theme.
- The first viewport contains a strong document/workspace hero and a large editor shell or connected-canvas proof.
- The editor shell has a sidebar, document pane, metadata, document rows, and at least one suggestion/comment/status detail.
- Lower sections preserve Paper through cards, feature miniatures, workflow rows, use-case panels, pricing, and footer.
- The layout is reviewed at 1600px with about 1440px primary content width.
- No section keeps a two-column split when text or cards become visibly compressed.
- Visible copy does not mention external recipe sources, internal files, Bricks, WordPress, JSON, conversion, or test harnesses.

## Final Critique Before Artifact Handoff

- Philosophy: does it feel like modern document-workspace software, not a generic SaaS page?
- Hierarchy: is the hero readable, calm, and paired with useful product proof?
- Execution: are borders, radii, spacing, buttons, cards, and miniatures consistent?
- Specificity: do the mockups and copy describe real document, editor, collaboration, or knowledge workflows?
- Restraint: is the palette light and quiet with accent used sparingly?
- Responsiveness: do nav, CTAs, editor shell, feature cards, workflow steps, pricing, and footer fit without overflow?
- Accessibility: does muted text remain readable and are controls keyboard/touch friendly?
- Bricks readiness: is the HTML semantic, local-first, and free of brittle text overlap?

## Repair Triggers

- Hero is a generic centered SaaS headline without editor proof.
- Product proof is a small dashboard thumbnail.
- Cards repeat the same layout without miniatures.
- A three/four-column grid squeezes copy below usable width.
- The page becomes dark because of system preference.
- Paper becomes beige scrapbook/editorial rather than product workspace.
