Field notes

Six rules that keep the page bold, useful, and readable.

The style is loud. The system is strict.

01

Pick one dominant entry point.

Hero, artifact, problem panel, and final CTA each get one obvious first read.

02

Use yellow for action and selection.

Yellow highlights the click path, selected chips, and page-state callouts.

03

Use violet for contrast slabs.

Violet creates a strong visual beat without taking over every component.

04

Make proof inspectable.

Rows, tabs, captions, and status chips make the object feel specific.

05

Keep one shadow direction.

The whole page uses down-right black shadows so depth feels intentional.

06

Carry the system below the fold.

Lower sections keep hard cards, labels, and explicit backgrounds.

FAQ

Questions before you press the loud button.

When does this style fit?

Use it when the page needs a strong point of view: launches, guides, pricing surfaces, creator products, or product campaigns.

What keeps it from becoming chaotic?

One dominant entry point per section, restrained accent use, readable copy, and consistent down-right shadows.

What should never change?

Flat fills, strong borders, visible focus states, high contrast, and enough spacing for the shadow system.

How does it translate to mobile?

Cards stack in content order, shadows shrink, CTAs stay touch-safe, and section backgrounds remain explicit.

Ready to build louder?
