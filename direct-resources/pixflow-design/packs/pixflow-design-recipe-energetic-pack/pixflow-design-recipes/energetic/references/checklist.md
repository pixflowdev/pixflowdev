## Quality Checks

- First viewport is recognizably energetic (thick borders, bold headlines, centered mockup, solid offset shadows), not generic SaaS.
- Desktop was reviewed at 1600px width with about 1440px content max.
- The page has a clear offer, active audience, and bold call to action.
- Typography is bold and expressive without clipping.
- All primary panels, buttons, and cards use thick 4px borders.
- Interactive elements use solid offset shadows (`4px 4px 0px 0px`) and have snappy hover scaling.
- No two-column section compresses a heading, paragraph, image frame, or card into an awkward skinny track.
- Monospaced fonts are used for details, badges, and labels.
- Mobile is polished, layout stacks correctly, and elements do not overflow.
- Color contrast meets WCAG 2.2 AA.
- Focus states are highly visible.
- Visible copy does not mention external recipe sources, test harnesses, Bricks conversion, or internal file names.

## Final Questions

- Would a cropped first-viewport screenshot communicate active/energetic style immediately?
- Do the interactive elements feel tactile and responsive?
- Do the hero and section intros use the full desktop canvas without squeezing the content?
- Are borders and shadows consistent across all containers?
- Did any lower section drift into thin borders, soft shadows, or generic layouts?
