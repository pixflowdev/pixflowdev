# Bento Critique

Use this after the first artifact draft and before handoff.

## First Viewport

- Is the first viewport immediately recognizable as a weighted bento board?
- Is the hero card clearly the largest and most important card?
- Does the hero card contain the main H1, lead, and primary action without needing text outside the grid?
- Do surrounding cards prove the product promise instead of decorating the hero?
- Is there a clear reading path from hero to proof to next action?

## Card Roles

- Does every card have one named job: hero, metric, capability, product mockup, checklist, quote, community, integration, workflow, or CTA?
- Are card sizes matched to importance?
- Are compact cards genuinely compact signals, not cramped paragraphs?
- Are product-specific labels present in mockups, checklists, and workflow cards?
- Are any cards repeated filler? If yes, remove or merge them.

## Product Evidence

- Is the main product mockup inspectable at desktop and mobile?
- Does the second board show workflow, automation, integrations, analytics, templates, or team controls relevant to the brief?
- Are metrics clearly placeholders unless real numbers were provided?
- Are testimonials role-based or generic unless real names were provided?
- Does the lower page still use bento language instead of becoming generic feature rows?

## Visual System

- Does the page preserve the active Pixflow Design design system?
- Are recipe fallback colors, fonts, radii, and shadows used only when no active design system is available?
- Is accent usage limited to one dominant accent plus restrained supporting tints?
- Are radii, borders, spacing, and shadows consistent enough to feel authored?
- Are saturated cards accessible on mobile?

## Width And Layout

- At 1600px, are cards wide enough for their content?
- Has the 12-column grid produced visible skinny columns?
- Are support cards packed intentionally around tall product mockups?
- Does the layout avoid orphan lower-row cards?
- Does mobile stack in a useful order without overflow?

## Repair Instructions

- If it looks like a generic card grid, enlarge the hero card and add more varied card spans.
- If it feels decorative, replace empty cards with product UI, checklists, metrics, integrations, or workflow states.
- If it feels noisy, reduce accent surfaces and make only one or two cards visually dominant.
- If the active design system is being ignored, restyle the cards through that system and keep only the bento composition method.
- If cards are cramped, add rows or split into a second board rather than shrinking text.
- If the preview looks like a component demo, expand it into a complete landing page with hero board, product proof board, trust/action card, CTA, and footer.
